Liouville theorem and Schrödinger equation

25 August 2021 Exotic expositions Quantum physics Classical physics Schrödinger equation

We start by proving Liouville theorem that the evolution of a classical system in the phase space is incompressible.

\(0\)\(\;=\;\)\(\dfrac{\eth \rho}{\eth t}\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial t}\)\(\,+\,\)\( \displaystyle\sum\limits^{N}_{n \;=\; 1}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}} \dfrac{\eth q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}{\eth t} \,+\, \dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \dfrac{\eth p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}{\eth t}}\right.\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}\)\(\dfrac{\eth q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}{\eth t}\)\(\,+\,\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}\)\(\dfrac{\eth p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}{\eth t}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}} \dfrac{\eth q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}{\eth t} \,+\, \dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \dfrac{\eth p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}{\eth t}}\right)\)

By Hamiltonian equations:

\(\;=\;\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial t}\)\(\,+\,\)\( \displaystyle\sum\limits^{N}_{n \;=\; 1}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}} \dfrac{\partial H}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \,-\, \dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \dfrac{\partial H}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}}\right.\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial H}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial H}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}} \dfrac{\partial H}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \,-\, \dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial p \vphantom{ p}_{n}} \dfrac{\partial H}{\partial q \vphantom{ q}_{n}}}\right)\)

At this point, we have to imagine what this relation looks like in the quantum world. To begin, \(\partial\)\(\rho\)\(\,/\,\)\(\partial\)\(q \vphantom{ q}_{n}\) can be considered as the component \(n\) of the density matrix under the \(q \vphantom{ q}_{n}\) basis, and \(\partial\)\(\hslash\)\(\,/\,\)\(\partial\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{n}\), the component \(n\) of the Hamiltonian matrix under the \(q \vphantom{ q}_{n}\) basis. Unfortunately, we no longer have \(p \vphantom{ p}_{n}\)\(,\,\)\(q \vphantom{ q}_{n}\) as a compatible set of coordinates; rather, they are off by a linear transformation.

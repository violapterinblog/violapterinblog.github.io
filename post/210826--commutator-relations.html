Commutator relations

26 August 2021 Exotic expositions Quantum physics Schrödinger equation

Schrödinger equation states a relation between wave function \(\psi\) and Hamiltonian \(H\):

Schrödinger: \(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial t}\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{t}\right.\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{t}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(H\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{t}\right.\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{t}\right]\)\(;\,\)

But it is unclear without empirical evidence what the expression of \(H\) should be.

Define the commutator:

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{A ,\, B}\right.\)\(A\)\(,\,\)\(B\)\(\left.\vphantom{A ,\, B}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(;\,\)

A simple approach is taking this commutation relation as an axiom between conjugate variables. For the position \(x\)\(,\,\)\(y\)\(,\,\)\(z\) and momentum \(p \vphantom{ p}_{x}\)\(,\,\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{y}\)\(,\,\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{z}\),

commutation_position: \(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{x}}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{x}\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{x}}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(;\,\)

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{y ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{y}}\right.\)\(y\)\(,\,\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{y}\)\(\left.\vphantom{y ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{y}}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(;\,\)

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{z ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{z}}\right.\)\(z\)\(,\,\)\(p \vphantom{ p}_{z}\)\(\left.\vphantom{z ,\, p \vphantom{ p}_{z}}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(;\,\)

The quantum harmonic oscillator

29 August 2021 Exotic expositions Quantum physics Schrödinger equation

To study the quantum counterpart of the classical harmonic oscillator, consider the one-dimentional Schrödinger equation \(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\hslash\)\(\left(\vphantom{\partial \,/\, \partial t}\right.\)\(\partial\)\(\,/\,\)\(\partial\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{\partial \,/\, \partial t}\right)\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(H\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right]\) with a quadratic potential:

\(H\)\(\psi\)\(\;\equiv\;\)\(\dfrac{\mathrm{d} \vphantom{ \mathrm{d}}^{2}}{\mathrm{d} x \vphantom{ x}^{2}}\)

If we accept such formal manipulation with matrix exponents:

\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(e \vphantom{ e}^{-\, \mathring{\imath} H t \,/\, \hslash}\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t \:\leftarrow\: 0}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\:\leftarrow\:\)\(0\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t \:\leftarrow\: 0}\right]\)

Then time-dependent Schrödinger equation can be reduced to the time-independent equation:

\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(e \vphantom{ e}^{-\, \mathring{\imath} H t \,/\, \hslash}\)\(\psi\)\(\left[\vphantom{x ,\, t \:\leftarrow\: 0}\right.\)\(x\)\(,\,\)\(t\)\(\:\leftarrow\:\)\(0\)\(\left.\vphantom{x ,\, t \:\leftarrow\: 0}\right]\)

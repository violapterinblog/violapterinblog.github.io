[4] The hydrogen atom

31 August 2021 Exotic expositions Quantum mechanics revisited Quantum physics Schrödinger equation

We continue our perfunctory review of quantum mechanics, and sketch the process of solving the wave function of electron in a hydrogen atom; it has not only historical significance, but the fine tructure also provides an instance of perturbation theory. In the presence of the Coulumb potential, with \(\zeta\) the charge and \(\mu\) the mass of proton (because \(m\) is used up for magnetic quantum numbers), the hydrogen atom has this Hamiltonian:

Hamiltonian:

\(H\)\(\;\equiv\;\)\(\dfrac{-\, \hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}}{2 \mu}\)\(\nabla \vphantom{ \nabla}^{2}\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\zeta}{4 \pi \varepsilon \vphantom{ \varepsilon}_{0}}\)\(\dfrac{1}{r}\)

By the formula of Laplacian in spherical coodinates:

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}}{2 \mu}\)\(\dfrac{1}{r \vphantom{ r}^{2}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\)\(r \vphantom{ r}^{2}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}}{2 \mu}\)\(\dfrac{1}{r \vphantom{ r}^{2}}\)\(\dfrac{1}{\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}}\right.\)\(\left(\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\,\mathrm{Sin}\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right)\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}}\right)\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}}{2 \mu}\)\(\dfrac{1}{r \vphantom{ r}^{2} \mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right] \vphantom{\mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right]}^{2}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial \vphantom{ \partial}^{2}}{\partial \varphi \vphantom{ \varphi}^{2}}\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\zeta}{4 \pi \varepsilon \vphantom{ \varepsilon}_{0}}\)\(\dfrac{1}{r}\)\(;\,\)

By symmetry, the wave function must have the form \(\psi\)\(\;=\;\)\(\rho\)\(\left[\vphantom{r}\right.\)\(r\)\(\left.\vphantom{r}\right]\)\(\chi\)\(\left[\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\varphi\)\(,\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right]\), where \(\varphi\) is azimuth and \(\vartheta\) zenith. Hence the time-independent Schrödinger equation reads:

\(H\)\(\rho\)\(\left[\vphantom{r}\right.\)\(r\)\(\left.\vphantom{r}\right]\)\(\chi\)\(\left[\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\varphi\)\(,\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(E\)\(\rho\)\(\left[\vphantom{r}\right.\)\(r\)\(\left.\vphantom{r}\right]\)\(\chi\)\(\left[\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\varphi\)\(,\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\varphi ,\, \vartheta}\right]\)\(;\,\)

We want to relate the expression of \(H\) with the total angular momentum \(L\). Here \(L\) is given as:

\(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\;=\;\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right.\)\(\mathbf{R}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\mathbf{P}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right)\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right.\)\(\mathbf{R}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\mathbf{P}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right)\)\(\psi\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla}\right.\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla}\right)\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla \psi}\right.\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla \psi}\right)\)

By noting \(\nabla\) either acts on the second \(\mathbf{r}\) or on \(\psi\) :

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(\left|\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla\right| \vphantom{ \left|\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla\right|}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(r \vphantom{ r}^{2}\)\(\nabla \vphantom{ \nabla}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(\left(\mathbf{r} \,\cdot\, \nabla\right) \vphantom{ \left(\mathbf{r} \,\cdot\, \nabla\right)}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)

Again by the formula of Laplacian in spherical coodinates:

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\)\(r \vphantom{ r}^{2}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\)\(\psi\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{1}{\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta} \psi}\right.\)\(\left(\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\,\mathrm{Sin}\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right)\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\psi\)\(\left.\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta} \psi}\right)\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{1}{\mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right] \vphantom{\mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right]}^{2}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial \vphantom{ \partial}^{2}}{\partial \varphi \vphantom{ \varphi}^{2}}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(\left(r \dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\right) \vphantom{ \left(r \dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\right)}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(r\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial r}\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{1}{\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\left(\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta} \psi}\right.\)\(\left(\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right.\)\(\,\mathrm{Sin}\,\)\(\vartheta\)\(\left.\vphantom{\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta}\right)\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta}\)\(\psi\)\(\left.\vphantom{\left(\,\mathrm{Sin}\, \vartheta\right) \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \vartheta} \psi}\right)\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{1}{\mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right] \vphantom{\mathrm{Sin} \left[\vartheta\right]}^{2}}\)\(\dfrac{\partial \vphantom{ \partial}^{2}}{\partial \varphi \vphantom{ \varphi}^{2}}\)\(\psi\)\(;\,\)

Now we observe a key fact:

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{L \vphantom{ L}^{2} ,\, H}\right.\)\(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\)\(,\,\)\(H\)\(\left.\vphantom{L \vphantom{ L}^{2} ,\, H}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(0\)

To see this, note that \(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\) are completely angular, which both commute with the two radial terms of \(H\) , and the two angular terms of \(H\) are just the same as \(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\), since both come from the Laplacian. Moreover, from the correspondence of angle and angular momentum,

\(L \vphantom{ L}_{z}\)\(\psi\)\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\hslash\)\(\mathring{\imath}\)\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \varphi}\)\(\psi\)\(;\,\)

And it can be seen that:

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{L \vphantom{ L}^{2} ,\, L \vphantom{ L}_{z}}\right.\)\(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\)\(,\,\)\(L \vphantom{ L}_{z}\)\(\left.\vphantom{L \vphantom{ L}^{2} ,\, L \vphantom{ L}_{z}}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(0\)\(;\,\)

\(\,\mathrm{Comm}\,\)\(\left[\vphantom{H ,\, L \vphantom{ L}_{z}}\right.\)\(H\)\(,\,\)\(L \vphantom{ L}_{z}\)\(\left.\vphantom{H ,\, L \vphantom{ L}_{z}}\right]\)\(\;=\;\)\(0\)\(;\,\)

Thus, we may assume that \(H\)\(,\,\)\(L \vphantom{ L}^{2}\)\(,\,\)\(L \vphantom{ L}_{z}\) share eigen states. We have concluded exactly the same from our discussion on commutator relations.

In fact, solving the radial part of Hamiltonian equation covers the one radial degree of freedom, and solving the angular momentum equation covers the two angular degree of freedom. Let us just do that.

[4] The hydrogen atom

31 August 2021 Exotic expositions Quantum mechanics revisited Quantum physics Schrödinger equation

We continue our perfunctory review of quantum mechanics, and sketch the process of solving the wave function of electron in a hydrogen atom; it has not only historical significance, but the fine tructure also provides an instance of perturbation theory. In the presence of the Coulumb potential, with \(\zeta\) the charge and \(\mu\) the mass of proton (because \(m\) is used up for magnetic quantum numbers), the hydrogen atom has this Hamiltonian:

\(H\)\(\;\equiv\;\)\(\dfrac{-\, \hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{2}}{2 \mu}\)\(\nabla \vphantom{ \nabla}^{2}\)\(\,-\,\)\(\dfrac{\zeta}{r}\)\(;\,\)

The time-independent Schrödinger equation reads

\(\hslash \vphantom{ \hslash}^{-\, 2}\)\(\mathbf{L} \vphantom{ \mathbf{L}}^{2}\)\(\;=\;\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right.\)\(\mathbf{R}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\mathbf{P}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right)\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right.\)\(\mathbf{R}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\mathbf{P}\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{R} \,\times\, \mathbf{P}}\right)\)\(\psi\)

By noting \(\nabla\) either acts on the second \(\mathbf{r}\) or on \(\psi\) :

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla}\right.\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla}\right)\)\(\left(\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla \psi}\right.\)\(\mathbf{r}\)\(\,\times\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)\(\left.\vphantom{\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla \psi}\right)\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(\left|\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla\right| \vphantom{ \left|\mathbf{r} \,\times\, \nabla\right|}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,-\,\)\(r\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)

\(\;=\;\)\(\,-\,\)\(r \vphantom{ r}^{2}\)\(\nabla \vphantom{ \nabla}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,+\,\)\(\left(\mathbf{r} \,\cdot\, \nabla\right) \vphantom{ \left(\mathbf{r} \,\cdot\, \nabla\right)}^{2}\)\(\psi\)\(\,-\,\)\(r\)\(\,\cdot\,\)\(\nabla\)\(\psi\)
